Ecodesign, Biodesign, and Circular Approaches

©POLI.Design Master in Design for Sustainability and Regeneration

This Master’s program is an educational project that spans a wide spectrum of approaches from sustainability to regeneration: from classical ecodesign strategies to regenerative design perspectives shaped by biodesign, multispecies thinking, and more-than-human design.

It aims to train a new generation of designers capable of critically responding to the environmental crisis and imagining systemic, relational, and ecologically responsible futures.

©POLI.Design Master in Design for Sustainability and Regeneration

The program responds to the urgent need to rethink production, consumption, and design practices in the face of climate change, biodiversity loss, ecosystem collapse, and resource depletion.

It challenges students to move beyond human-centeredness, considering the needs of ecosystems, living beings, and non-human actors in every stage of the design process.

©POLI.Design Master in Design for Sustainability and Regeneration

Through theoretical insights and hands-on experiences, participants explore sustainability as a multi-dimensional challenge, engaging with tools, strategies, and methods that foster awareness, responsibility, and transformative design action.

By bridging systemic knowledge and material experimentation, the Master equips students to become agents of transition, able to create positive environmental, social, and economic impact across different sectors and degrees of intervention.
